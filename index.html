Heat Capacity

The thermal or heat capacity (Cp) of a body is the quantity of heat required to raise the temperature of that body by 1 kelvin or 1°celsius

NB:Temperature of the whole body not a unit mass, that is what differentiates it from specific heat capacity as we shall see

Also, Mathematically:
Heat Capacity (Cp) = Mass (m) × Specific Heat Capacity (C)
Cp=mC

Specific Heat Capacity

The specific heat capacity (C) of a body is the quantity of heat required to raise the temperature of a unit mass of that body by 1 kelvin or 1°celsius

NB:Temperature of a unit mass not of the whole body, these facts are to be remembered at all times

Also, Mathematically:
Specific heat Capacity (C) = Heat Capacity (Cp) ÷ Mass (m)

Summary

So we learned a few things;

  • Heat capacity (Cp) of a body is the quantity of heat required to raise the temperature of that body 1 kelvin or 1°celsius
  • The specific heat capacity (C) of a body is the quantity of heat required to raise the temperature of a unit mass of that body by 1 kelvin or 1°celsius
  • The calorimeter is used to measure specific heat capacity
  • Good conducting materials like copper and aluminium are used in the method of mixtures experiment so as to minimize heat loss
  • The principle guiding the method of mixtures procedure is that the heat lost by the hotter material(s) is/are always equal to the heat gained by the colder material(s)
